Our Heritage Story

Preserving the ancient art of handloom weaving for over three generations

Three Generations of Excellence

Heritage Handlooms was founded in 1952 by Master Craftsman Ravi Sharma in the heart of Rajasthan. What began as a small family workshop has grown into a celebrated name in authentic Indian textiles, yet we've never lost sight of our roots.

Our journey started when Ravi ji learned the intricate art of handloom weaving from his grandfather, who was a royal court weaver. The techniques, patterns, and dedication to quality have been passed down through generations, ensuring that every piece we create carries the soul of traditional Indian craftsmanship.

Today, under the guidance of his grandson Arjun Sharma, Heritage Handlooms continues to honor this legacy while embracing sustainable practices and supporting local artisan communities across India.

Our Handloom Process

From cotton to creation - the journey of our textiles

1

Cotton Selection

We source the finest quality cotton from certified organic farms, ensuring purity and sustainability from the very beginning.

2

Spinning & Dyeing

Cotton is spun into yarn using traditional methods, then dyed with natural colors using time-honored techniques passed down through generations.

3

Handloom Weaving

Master weavers create intricate patterns on traditional handlooms, with each piece taking days or weeks to complete depending on complexity.

4

Block Printing

Traditional wooden blocks are used to print intricate designs, with each color requiring a separate block and printing process.

5

Quality Control

Every piece undergoes rigorous quality checks to ensure it meets our high standards before being carefully packaged for our customers.
